Spiritual Journey


My story is like many other stories of individuals who have grown up in the church and then struggled to make their faith their own. All throughout my life I have been an active participant in the life and community of the church. As a child and into my teenage years, I was never forced to attend church on Sunday mornings or youth group on Wednesday and Sunday nights. It was something I wanted and chose to do. Most of my relationships and my friend circles were also in the church, which certainly influenced my decision. There is not one clear moment in my memory where I recall dedicating my life to Christ, but several similar moments.


Call to Ministry


I remember being on a church retreat where I spent that entire weekend just trying to surrender my confusion and frustration to God and asking for some sort of direction. When I returned home and spent some time reflecting on the weekend and on the summer that I felt it. What I realized was that I am very limited as a teacher, but where showed strength and where I found the most joy in teaching were things that a pastor does: walking, learning, and growing with people. I approached my friends, family, and pastor with this revelation and they all recognized these traits in me and truly affirmed my pursuit in ministry, and this has continued to be affirmed throughout my time in seminary.
